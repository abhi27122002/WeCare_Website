What is addiction

Symptoms || Treatment || Helpful Organization
Addiction is an inability to stop using a substance or engaging in a behavior even though it is causing psychological and physical harm.

The term addictionTrusted Source does not only refer to dependence on substances such as heroin or cocaine. Some addictions also involve an inability to stop partaking in activities such as gambling, eating, or working.

Addiction is a chronic condition that can also result from taking medications. In fact, the misuse of opioids — particularly illicitly made fentanyl — caused nearly 50,000 deathsTrusted Source in the United States in 2019 alone.

Symptoms

The primary indications of addiction are:

1] declining grades or difficulty at school

2] poor performance at work

3] relationship difficulties, which often involve lashing out at people who identify the addiction

4] an inability to stop using a substance even though it may be causing health problems or personal problems, such as issues with employment or relationships

5] a noticeable lack of energy in daily activities

6] profound changes in appearance, including weight loss and a noticeable abandonment of hygiene

7] appearing defensive when asked about substance use

Treatment

Medicinal advances and progress in diagnosis have helped the medical community develop various ways to manage and resolve addictionTrusted Source.

Some methods include:

1] medication-based treatment

2] behavioral therapy and counseling

3] medical devices to treat withdrawal

4] treating related psychological factors, such as depression

Addiction treatment is highly personalized and often requires the support of the individual’s community or family.

Treatment can take a long time and may be complicated. Addiction is a chronic condition with a range of psychological and physical effects. Each substance or behavior may require different management techniques.

Helpful organizations and hotlines

A person with addiction can find many organizations that may help them. A person can also call a hotline for help with their addiction.

The following organizations can be helpful for a person with addiction:

1] To Write Love on Her Arms: This organization is dedicated to helping people with addiction, self-harm, depression, and suicide.

2] Shatterproof: This organization provides educational resources and community alliances.

3] Faces & Voices of Recovery: This organization is dedicated to supporting and helping people with addiction, their families, and their friends.

4] The Amy Winehouse Foundation: This organization provides addiction support and music therapy.
